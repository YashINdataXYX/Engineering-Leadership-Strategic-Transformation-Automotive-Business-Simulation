## What This Project Is

A competitive business simulation modelling the automotive industry — 10 companies, 10 decision rounds, real market dynamics including demand shifts, inflation shocks, oil price changes, and EV regulatory pressure.

My role: Technical & Engineering Executive. Every decision had a financial consequence traced through company reports issued each round.

---

## Strategic Decisions Made

### Automation
Invested £1,015M cumulatively across 10 rounds. Early investment built the foundation; integration with smart manufacturing principles in later rounds delivered compound productivity gains.

- Round 1: £5M total automation spend, productivity 41.5 cars/worker/year  
- Round 8: £715M cumulative, productivity peaks at 70.3 cars/worker/year  
- Round 10: £1,015M cumulative, productivity index 1.92

### Product Portfolio Expansion
Grew from 2 models to 5 by entering new segments strategically:

| Model | Segment | Launch | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| ixUV | Compact Hybrid SUV | Round 1 (existing) | Stable volume |
| mxUV | Full-Size Hybrid SUV | Round 1 (existing) | Stable volume |
| Regalia | Luxury | Round 3 | 15–18% consistent |
| Ionex | Electric Vehicle | Round 6 | 5% (R6) → 12% (R9) |
| Zesta | Small Economy | Round 8 | Competitive pricing |

### R&D Allocation
R&D spending was sequenced: early rounds funded design quality and safety improvements to reduce warranty costs; later rounds funded EV development and manufacturing innovation.

- Warranty cost fell from £600+ per unit to £449.78 by Round 10  
- Ionex grew from 45,000 units (Round 6) to 80,000 units (Round 9)

### Workforce Management
Scaling headcount too fast without matching automation caused a productivity dip in Round 3 (43.12 → 38.88 cars/worker). Corrected by reducing to 3,550 in Round 5 while increasing automation. Productivity recovered to 61.7 by Round 5.

## Key Decisions and Trade-offs

**Ionex EV launch in Round 6**  
Risk: Margins started at 5%, dragging overall profitability. Reason to proceed: EEA regulatory trajectory made EV capability non-negotiable; early entry built a competitive advantage before competitors. Outcome: 12% margins and 80,000 units by Round 9.

**Workforce reduction in Rounds 4–5**  
Counter-intuitive but necessary. Rapid hiring in Rounds 1–3 outpaced automation readiness. Reducing to 3,550 employees and investing in automation instead restored efficiency before the next scale-up.

**Regalia as a margin stabiliser**  
The luxury model's 15–18% margins funded higher-risk investments in EV and economy segments. Portfolio balance, not single-model dominance, drove sustained profitability.

## Frameworks Applied

- **Industry 4.0 maturity model** — adoption from basic automation to smart factory integration mirrors real adoption curves
- **Four-pillar performance model** — Productivity, Innovation/R&D, Market/Portfolio, Sustainability
- **Leading and lagging indicators** — automation spend and design scores (leading) tracked alongside profit and warranty costs (lagging)
- **Agile R&D sequencing** — iterative investment in Ionex design rather than large upfront spend
